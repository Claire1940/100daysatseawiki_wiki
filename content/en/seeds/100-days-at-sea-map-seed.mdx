<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding the 100 Days At Sea Map System">

Navigating the vast expanse of the ocean in **100 Days At Sea** requires more than just a sturdy raft; it requires a deep understanding of the world layout. While many players search for a specific **100 days at sea map seed**, it is important to clarify that the current version of the game features a unified world environment. Instead of random seeds, the game utilizes a fixed-coordinate system for its major landmarks, including the mysterious islands and high-threat biomes.

The map is designed around the central theme of the Bermuda Triangle. As you sail, you will encounter various island types that scale in difficulty based on their distance from the starting zone. Understanding the "seed" of your run really comes down to the class you choose and how you route your progression through these islands.

Most players should focus on the "Open-Sea Island Route" first. This involves staying within a reasonable distance of the raft while using the harpoon to hook floating resources. Once you have stabilized your food and water supplies, you can begin pushing toward the major boss locations.

<Section icon={Anchor} title="Raft Base Building and Navigation Steps">

Your raft is your mobile home and the only safe haven on the map. As you explore further from the center, your base must evolve to handle the increased pressure from sea threats.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Resource Hooking">
    Stay on your starter platform and use the **Harpoon** to pull in floating wood and scraps. Do not jump into the water unnecessarily, as stamina and oxygen are limited early on.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Bonfire Foundation">
    Upgrade your **Bonfire** as quickly as possible. Reaching Bonfire level 5 marks the transition to the mid-game, providing better light and unlocking new structure options.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Expansion and Storage">
    Build out your raft horizontally to create space for **Storage Sacks** and a **Cooking Pot**. Cooking chowder is essential for maintaining hunger levels during long voyages.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defensive Fortification">
    As you enter the Open Ocean, add defensive structures. Ensure you have clear "combat lanes" so you can move freely to repel raids while keeping your essential structures protected.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Boss Preparation">
    Before heading to the Volcano or Ice regions, ensure your raft has enough food and healing supplies. Your raft serves as the launch point for these high-stakes missions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Base Management">
Avoid over-expanding your raft before you have the resources to defend it. A massive raft is harder to protect during multi-directional raids. Focus on functional density rather than pure size.
</Callout>

</Section>
