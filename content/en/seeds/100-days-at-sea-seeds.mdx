<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Core Food & Farming Mechanics">

Hunger and thirst decay rapidly in 100 Days At Sea. Consuming raw food items like raw potatoes or raw fish provides minimal nutrition and can lead to rapid health depletion. To establish a sustainable source of food, you must transition from gathering floating debris to building specialized agricultural and cooking structures on your raft.

The table below outlines the primary food items available in the game, their sources, and their overall utility:

| Food Item | Source | Hunger Restored | Best Use |
| Raw Potato | Floating crates / Island foraging | Low | Emergency food only / Save for cooking |
| Crackers | Floating crates | Medium | Great early-game quick snack |
| Raw Crab Meat | Killing crabs on islands | Low | Do not eat raw / Cook into chowder |
| Seagull Meat | Shooting birds with harpoon | Medium | Cook on campfire or cooking pot |
| Fish | Fishing rod in open water | Medium | Cook into sushi or chowder |
| Chowder | Cooking pot recipe | High | Best overall food for long exploration |

To process these raw ingredients into high-nutrition meals, you must build key structures on your raft. Prioritize these structures in the early days of your survival run:

| Structure | Materials Required | Pearl/Coin Cost | Core Utility |
| Campfire | Starter item | Free | Basic cooking and warmth at night |
| Cooking Pot | Wood planks, Metal scrap | Coins / Crafting | Prepares high-tier food like chowder |
| Crab Trap | Wood planks, Scrap metal | Coins / Crafting | Automates crab meat collection |
| Fishing Rod | Starter item | Free | Manual food gathering from the raft |

<Callout type="warning" title="Dehydration Threat">
Thirst depletes significantly faster than hunger. If your thirst bar hits zero, your health will collapse rapidly. Always build a water purifier alongside your food setup.
</Callout>
