<Section icon={Shield} title="Raft Base Building and Bonfire Progression">

Your raft is a mobile fortress. In any effective **100 days at sea walkthrough**, the progression of your bonfire acts as the primary gatekeeper for new technology. As you upgrade the bonfire, you unlock defensive structures and utility items that allow you to venture further into the Volcano and Ice biomes.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Hook Resources Early">
    Use the harpoon to pull in floating objects. This saves health and keeps you safe from sea threats during the early game. Focus on wood and metal scraps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Functional Expansion">
    Build your raft outward to create dedicated zones for storage, cooking, and combat. A cluttered raft leads to mistakes during night raids.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bonfire Upgrades">
    Invest your gathered materials into the Bonfire. Reaching Level 5 is a major milestone that signals you are ready for the mid-game transition.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Food and Healing">
    Craft the Cooking Pot and start making Chowder. This is the most reliable way to manage hunger and health regeneration during long voyages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Base building must prioritize defense. As you reach higher day counts, the frequency of raids increases. Positioning your bonfire in the center of the raft and surrounding it with defensive walls is a standard meta-strategy. Ensure you leave "combat lanes"—clear paths where you can move freely to engage enemies without tripping over storage chests.

| Bonfire Level | Unlockable Benefit | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Basic Crafting | Starter Materials |
| **Level 5** | Mid-Tier Defenses | Wood, Plastic, Metal |
| **Level 9** | Advanced Tech | Rare Island Resources |
| **Master** | Challenge Badge | Max Level before Day 3 |

<Callout type="tip" title="Building Tip">
Never build your storage crates on the very edge of the raft. If a sea monster or raid damages that specific tile, you risk losing all the resources stored in that chest. Always keep your valuables at least one tile inward from the perimeter.
</Callout>

</Section>

The Raider and Fire Mage are considered the "end-game" classes due to their high Pearl costs and massive damage output. However, players should not ignore the utility of the Sailor or Merchant for specific economy-focused runs. If you are playing in a duo, having one Medic and one Sharpshooter creates a balanced team capable of taking down the Squid Boss with ease.
