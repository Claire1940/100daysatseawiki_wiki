<Section icon={Target} title="How to Catch the 100 Days At Sea Fish Sandwich">

Acquiring the **100 days at sea fish sandwich** requires a proper fishing setup. While you start your journey with a basic harpoon, you will need to craft a Fishing Rod to begin harvesting the ocean's bounty. Fishing is a simple yet highly rewarding mechanic that can yield standard fish, rare ingredients, and even fully prepared meals like the fish sandwich.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Gather Starter Materials">
    Use your harpoon to hook floating wood planks and debris from your raft. You will need to process these raw materials at your grinder to create building components.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Craft the Fishing Rod">
    Open your raft bench build menu and craft a Fishing Rod. The standard recipe requires **5 Wood**, **3 Rope**, and **1 Hook**. Ensure you have sufficient inventory space before crafting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate Deep Water Ripples">
    Equip your Fishing Rod and look for active ripples in the water. While you can fish anywhere, casting your line directly into active ripples increases your catch rate and chances of pulling rare items.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Catch">
    Click or tap to cast your line, wait for the bobber to dip beneath the surface, and reel in the catch. With a bit of luck, you will pull a fully prepared fish sandwich directly from the sea.
  </Step>
</Steps>

In addition to direct fishing, you can also interact with the Fisherman NPC found on shallow islands. Completing his mandatory trades and delivering specific fish types can reward you with high-value food items, recipes, and utility tools.

<Callout type="info" title="Fishing Mechanics">
Fishing during the night is often safer and highly efficient because you are less distracted by standard daytime raft maintenance. However, keep your guard up as night raids can still occur while you are fishing.
</Callout>

</Section>
