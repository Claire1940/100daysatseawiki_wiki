Surviving the Bermuda Triangle requires a balance of resource gathering, raft expansion, and strategic positioning. In this guide, we will break down how to establish a successful **100 days at sea outpost** to secure your progression, maximize your resource multipliers, and survive the brutal night raids. The gameplay in this open-world Roblox experience by Stranded Devs is split into distinct day and night cycles. An outpost or rival rig acts as a permanent anchor point in the open ocean. Instead of constantly sailing back to the starter island, establishing a staging base near these outposts allows you to process materials, restock hunger and thirst bars, and safely store your loot. Hostile outposts, known as Rival Rigs, feature radio towers and high-tier guards that must be cleared to claim rare weapons and doubloons.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Choosing the Best Seed: Outpost Proximity & Resource Layouts">

In 100 Days At Sea, the world generation is governed by custom seed numbers. Seeds control the layout of the ocean, the placement of the eight biomes, and the distance between islands. Choosing a high-quality seed is the single most important step before committing to a long-term run. The best seeds cluster container spawns, starter islands, and outposts close together, minimizing the travel distance required to transport heavy building materials.

When selecting a seed, prioritize layouts that position a peaceful trading outpost or a resource-dense shipwreck island within a short sailing distance of your spawn point. This allows you to quickly establish your **100 days at sea outpost** and grind the necessary wood and stone before hunger and thirst bars deplete.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Staging Base & Raft Building: Setting Up Near Outposts">

Raft building and base expansion are the keys to long-term survival. Your starter raft is highly vulnerable to shark attacks and pirate boarding parties. To turn your mobile platform into a fortified **100 days at sea outpost**, you must prioritize functional structures over decorative pieces. Focus on expanding the raft floor, building defensive walls, and placing essential crafting stations like the cooking pot and water purifier.

When staging near a hostile Rival Rig or a high-tier biome like the Volcano or Ice region, your base must serve as a safe haven. Keep your combat lanes clear, store your overflow loot in container shelters or sacks, and ensure your bonfire is upgraded to provide warmth and healing buffs.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Classes & Companions: Best Loadouts for Outpost Raiding">

Unlocking the right classes and companions is essential for clearing hostile outposts and surviving past Day 50. Classes are special loadouts purchased with Pearls, providing unique passive buffs and starter tools. Companions are pets rolled from Pearl chests that automate chores or provide massive combat multipliers.

When raiding a Rival Rig, you need a class with high durability or ranged damage. The Survivor class is excellent for early-to-mid game survival due to slower hunger drain and extra oxygen. For late-game outpost raiding, combat-focused classes like the Pirate, Raider, or Fire Mage dominate the meta.
