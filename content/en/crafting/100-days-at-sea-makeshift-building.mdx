<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Crafting Recipes and Material Requirements">

Crafting in 100 Days At Sea flows through a simple progression chain: gathering raw debris with your harpoon, grinding it down, and assembling structures at your workbench. To construct a **100 days at sea makeshift building**, you need a steady supply of basic materials. Unlike the advanced late-game Container Shelter, which requires heavy iron and rope stockpiles, makeshift structures are designed to be cheap, accessible, and fast to erect.

The table below outlines the core crafting recipes you will need during your first few days on the ocean to establish a functional base.

| Structure / Item | Materials Required | Primary Purpose | Crafting Station |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wooden Raft** | 10 Logs / Wood | Expands the floating base platform | Starter Build Menu |
| **Grinder** | 10 Scrap Metal + 5 Stone | Breaks raw debris into usable materials | Workbench |
| **Makeshift Shelter** | 15 Logs + 10 Scrap Metal | Unlocks the early day multiplier | Workbench |
| **Container Shelter** | 20 Scrap Metal + 10 Wood | Full weather protection from rain and cold | Advanced Workbench |
| **Cooking Pot** | 10 Scrap Metal + 5 Wood | Cooks nourishing chowder to manage hunger | Workbench |

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management Warning">
Do not jump straight into building advanced shelters. If you spend all your scrap metal on a Container Shelter before setting up a Grinder or a Cooking Pot, your hunger and thirst bars will collapse before you can even utilize the weather protection. Always prioritize food and water stations first.
</Callout>

By focusing on makeshift building pieces first, you save valuable scrap metal and wood that can be redirected toward weapons and tools, such as the Flintlock Pistol or Iron Sword, which are vital for surviving night raids.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Construction Guide">

Establishing your first secure shelter can be confusing if you do not prioritize your tasks. Follow this optimized step-by-step walkthrough to construct your **100 days at sea makeshift building** efficiently during your first session.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete the Lobby Tutorial">
    Before the ocean world generates, complete the quick lobby tutorial. Gather beach scrap and feed it into the grinder to fill the progress bar. Once finished, craft your starter raft and board immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Harpoon Driftwood and Metal Scrap">
    Stay on your raft and use your harpoon to hook floating crates, planks, and barrels. Avoid swimming far from the raft early on, as sharks can quickly deplete your health bar. Feed these raw materials into your raft's grinder.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build a Workbench and Basic Platform">
    Expand your raft's flooring to create a stable working area. Craft a Workbench and place it in a centralized location. This unlocks the build menu for advanced survival tools like the Cooking Pot and basic weapons.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Erect the Makeshift Shelter">
    Once you have accumulated at least 15 logs and 10 scrap metal, open your build menu. Select the Makeshift Shelter and place it on your reinforced floor tiles. This immediately triggers your active day multiplier.
  </Step>
</Steps>
