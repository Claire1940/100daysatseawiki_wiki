<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: The Path to Day 100">

Surviving 100 days requires a disciplined approach to base building and exploration. You cannot simply sail into the sunset and hope for the best; you need a structured plan that balances safety with progression.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Harpoon Phase">
    Focus entirely on the harpoon for the first 5 days. Do not leave your raft. Pull in every crate and plank to build a 3x3 platform and a basic campfire.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Food and Water Loop">
    Build a Cooking Pot as soon as possible. Use the "We Cookin" method to craft Chowder. This stabilizes your hunger and allows you to survive the first major night raid.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Island Scouting">
    Once you have a basic weapon (Machete or Flintlock), visit the nearest island. Look for chests to find the **100 days at sea defibrillator** or enough Pearls to buy one.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Boss Preparation">
    Fortify your raft with defenses. Upgrade your bonfire to Level 5. Before attempting the Squid Boss, ensure your team has a designated healer and at least two sets of ranged weapons.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Final Stretch">
    From Day 70 to Day 100, play defensively. Avoid unnecessary island trips. Focus on maintaining your raft's integrity and keeping your food stocks high.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Raft Integrity">
As you reach later days, raids become more frequent. If your raft is destroyed, the run ends regardless of your health. Always prioritize raft repairs over individual gear upgrades in the late game.
</Callout>

</Section>
