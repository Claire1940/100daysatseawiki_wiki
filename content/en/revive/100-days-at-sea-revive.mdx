<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Raft Building Guide">

Reviving a failing run often comes down to poor base architecture. If your raft is too small, you lack storage; if it is too large too early, you cannot defend the perimeter. Follow this progression to ensure your **100 days at sea revive** efforts lead to a sustainable fortress.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Resource Hooking">
    Stay on your starter platform. Use the **Harpoon** to pull in every floating crate. Do not jump into the water unless absolutely necessary, as sharks can end your run instantly. Focus on wood and scrap metal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The 3x3 Expansion">
    Expand your raft to at least a 3x3 grid. This provides enough space for a **Bonfire**, a **Cooking Pot**, and two **Storage Chests**. Keep your Bonfire in the center to maximize light coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bonfire Upgrading">
    Invest your gathered materials into upgrading the Bonfire. High-level bonfires are required for certain survival badges and provide better protection against the cold in the Ice Biome.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defensive Fortification">
    Once you reach Day 20, start placing walls and spikes. Raids become more frequent and involve enemies that can board your raft. Having a clear "combat lane" allows you to kite enemies while using ranged weapons.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Base Safety">
Never build your storage chests on the outer edge of the raft. If a sea monster destroys that specific tile, you will lose all the items stored within that chest.
</Callout>
