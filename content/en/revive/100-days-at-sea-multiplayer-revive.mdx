<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Multiplayer Survival">

To ensure you never actually need a desperate **100 days at sea multiplayer revive**, your team should follow a strict progression loop. Disorganization is the leading cause of raft wipes in the Bermuda Triangle. By assigning tasks and following a set path, you can keep the group's health high and resources plentiful.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Resource Split">
    Upon spawning, have two players focus entirely on the **Harpoon** to pull in floating debris. The other two should immediately start placing the first set of raft expansions to ensure there is enough standing room for everyone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Medkit Rush">
    Prioritize unlocking the **Medic** class or finding Medkits in island chests. Having at least one Medkit on the raft by Day 5 is essential for surviving the first major night raid.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bonfire Leveling">
    The Bonfire is your central hub. Leveling it to **Bonfire 5** as a team unlocks better cooking options and defense structures. Assign one player to manage the fire while others explore.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Coordinated Island Raids">
    When visiting mysterious islands, never go alone. Use a "buddy system" where a combat-focused class (like the Swordsman) is always paired with a support class (like the Medic) to handle sudden enemy ambushes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Day 100 Final Push">
    As you approach the final days, stockpile Chowder and Medkits. The difficulty of raids increases, and your ability to quickly recover health will be the only thing standing between you and the **Rescued!** badge.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Danger Zone">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Multiplayer Raft Building and Defense">

In a multiplayer setting, your raft needs to be more than just a floating platform; it must be a functional mobile base that supports multiple players. A cramped raft leads to players getting stuck during combat, which often results in unnecessary damage. Building with "combat lanes" and clear paths to the Bonfire is vital for effective **100 days at sea multiplayer revive** maneuvers.

| Structure Type | Purpose in Multiplayer | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonfire** | Central healing and progression hub | **Extreme** |
| **Cooking Pot** | Mass-producing Chowder for the squad | High |
| **Storage Chests** | Organizing shared resources and ammo | High |
| **Defensive Walls** | Protecting the Medic during raids | Medium |
| **Expansion Tiles** | Providing enough space for 4 players to fight | Medium |

When building, ensure the **Cooking Pot** is easily accessible. Cooking Chowder provides essential health and hunger restoration. In multiplayer, one player can be the dedicated "Chef," ensuring that everyone's inventory is stocked with at least 3-5 food items before any major voyage.
