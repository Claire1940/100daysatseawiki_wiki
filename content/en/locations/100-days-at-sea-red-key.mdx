<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Harpoon Phase">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Base Stabilization">
    Do not explore islands until your raft has a Bonfire and a Storage Crate. A stable base allows you to retreat and recover health when island raids go wrong.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Raft Building and Bonfire Progression">

Your raft is your sanctuary. In 100 Days At Sea, building aimlessly will lead to a cluttered deck that makes combat difficult. Following a structured build path ensures you have the resources and space to handle the escalating difficulty of the Bermuda Triangle.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Functional Foundation">
    Expand your raft to at least a 5x5 grid. This provides enough space for a Bonfire, a Cooking Pot, and at least three Storage Crates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Bonfire Milestones">
    The Bonfire is your primary progression marker. Upgrading it to Level 5 unlocks better crafting recipes, while Level 9 is required for late-game survival against the Squid Boss.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Defensive Layering">
    Once you have a gun (found via the **Red Key** or chests), build defensive walls around your Bonfire. During night raids, enemies will target the fire first.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Structure | Requirement | Benefit | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonfire Lvl 1** | Starter | Basic light and heat | Mandatory |
| **Cooking Pot** | Scrap / Wood | Craft Chowder for health | High |
| **Storage Crate** | Wood | Save resources between runs | High |
| **Reinforced Walls** | Iron / Wood | Protects against shark attacks | Medium |
| **Jumbo Yacht** | Millionaire Class | Massive mobile base | Low |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Build Note">
Do not over-expand your raft width early. A wider raft is harder to defend from multiple angles during the Ghost Galleon raids. Focus on verticality or centralizing your most important tools.
</Callout>
