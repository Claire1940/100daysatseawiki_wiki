<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Survival Guide for Crewmates">

Surviving 100 days in the ocean requires a balance of resource gathering, base building, and combat preparation. Whether you are playing solo or coordinating with a team, following a structured progression path prevents critical mistakes that can end your run early.

Using the **Crewmate** class gives you a distinct advantage during the difficult early days. The starting Flintlock allows you to eliminate sharks and hostile island mobs before they can damage your raft or deplete your health. Use this ranged advantage to secure high-value resource zones.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Hook Resources with the Harpoon">
    Focus on pulling floating debris toward your raft using the harpoon. Do not jump into the deep water early, as sea creatures can quickly deplete your health. Keep gathering until you have enough wood, metal, and cloth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build a Compact Raft Base">
    Construct a stable platform around your central bonfire. Place storage chests, a cooking pot, and defensive walls. A compact layout is much easier to defend during night raids when waves of enemies attack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Upgrade Your Bonfire to Level 5">
    Feed fuel and resources to your bonfire to reach Level 5. This unlocks advanced crafting options, increases your base's overall warmth, and expands your light radius, keeping nighttime threats at bay.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Sail to Islands for Pearl Chests">
    Once your raft is stable, navigate to nearby islands. Search for unopened chests to farm Pearls. These Pearls are the key to unlocking premium classes like the **Millionaire**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Harpoon Efficiency">
Always aim your harpoon at glowing blue debris. These crates yield high-tier materials like metal and cloth, which are essential for early bonfire upgrades.
</Callout>

</Section>
